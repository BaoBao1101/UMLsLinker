A 61-year-old female patient with severe chronic lumbar pain, functional impairment, and grade II anterolisthesis of L4 underwent lumbar interbody fusion. A custom-designed trabecular titanium cage was implanted, featuring an optimized porous structure for enhanced fixation and bone ingrowth. To further promote bone regeneration, a biocomposite hydrogel synthesized from adipose-derived stromal-vascular fraction and BMP-2 was applied. Postoperative assessment demonstrated significant pain reduction, improved functional activity, and early bone fusion formation. Radiological imaging confirmed stable implant positioning, progressive trabecularization, and successful osseointegration. No complications, such as implant migration or material loosening, were observed. Patient A: a 61-year-old individual with a body mass index (BMI) of 33.6 kg/m² (height 161 cm, weight 87 kg), was admitted to the clinic with complaints of severe lower back pain radiating to the right lower limb, which worsened with movement. The pain had been present for 3 years. Initially, pharmacological therapy and chiropractic treatment provided partial relief, but over time, the pain progressively worsened and the treatment became ineffective. The patient was also diagnosed with bilateral osteoarthritis of the knee joints (grade II-III) and arterial hypertension.
