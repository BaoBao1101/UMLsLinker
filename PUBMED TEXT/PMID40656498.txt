Four-day postpartum, a 34-year-old female presented with severe paraplegia (1/5 motor power), complete T12–L1 sensory loss, and bowel incontinence. The lumbar magnetic resonance imaging (MRI) revealed a subacute subdural hematoma extending from the L1 to L5 levels. An urgent L1–L5 laminectomy was performed for clot evacuation. Within 3 postoperative months, she regained 4+/5 motor strength bilaterally and full bowel/bladder control.
