A male neonate was delivered at term via normal vaginal delivery, weighing 3.1 kg and with an APGAR (Appearance, Pulse, Grimace, Activity, Respiration) score of 9/10. Prenatal history was significant for the absence of maternal folic acid supplementation. On the second day of life, the infant was admitted to the Emergency Department for evaluation of a lumbar spinal defect. No additional medical or family history was reported.
